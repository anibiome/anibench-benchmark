# Anti-gaming contract

AniBench releases must prove the following attacks fail:

- rename a study, sponsor, institution, journal, or acronym;
- repeat impressive assay, randomization, or publication keywords;
- duplicate files, rows, timepoints, features, assays, or modality aliases;
- split one assay into many named subpanels;
- use enrolled N for a small assayed subset;
- use an epsilon-quality, one-target full-cohort assay to unlock population,
  personalization, or reuse credit;
- borrow parent-trial sites, countries, arms, controls, or assignment quality for
  a nested assay subset;
- join unmatched people, specimens, or dates;
- unlock linked functional credit with a zero-information biological partner or
  without an explicit functional-to-biological joint denominator;
- permute dates, people, interventions, or labels;
- relabel high-frequency rows as independent timepoints;
- present a redundant modality as an orthogonal biological direction;
- call clinician discretion an adaptive randomized policy;
- call a retrospective subgroup personalized intervention;
- claim public access without a successful clean execution;
- use one generic access receipt to authorize unrelated modalities or claims;
- add arbitrary high-valued component fields, delete hard fields, or rename
  component aliases to manipulate an average;
- forge source, evidence-cell, rating, deep-lock, or release-lock values and then
  recompute the packet content ID;
- mutate a chart, source span, or formula after scoring;
- use an ANI study to fit the reference or weights.
- declare assignment or context counts larger than linked retained outcome support;
- duplicate one participant-event lineage with a microscopic retention change;
- split different modalities from one canonical participant-event support across
  separate joint bundles to discard cross-modal covariance or multiply event counts;
- buy causal or transport credit when the linked observer is unknown;
- call a fixed-probability moderator analysis a personalized policy;
- combine the best rank, strength, and support from incompatible assignment stages;
- aggregate stages with different outcome geometry or moderator identities merely
  because their matrix ranks match;
- treat cluster or crossover participants as independent without ICC, period,
  carryover, and washout geometry;
- declare an unbounded rate process without a window and event-count model;
- add a sub-machine-epsilon resource cost that disappears from Pareto arithmetic;
- combine sibling list insertions/removals whose index shifts make the mutation
  result depend on application order;
- use source pointers or modality labels to synthesize external observation or
  covariance matrices.

Expected results live in executable unit, property, adversarial, and golden-study tests. A release cannot replace those tests with a prose statement that the benchmark is robust.

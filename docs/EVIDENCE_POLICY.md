# AniBench v2 evidence policy

## Five evidence lanes

- **Design Preview**: author-committed prospective geometry.
- **Registered Protocol**: frozen registry or protocol geometry.
- **Realized**: measured, linked, retained, and quality-controlled geometry.
- **Accessible**: successful independent execution over lawful data access.
- **Demonstrated**: held-out biological learning with uncertainty, null, and transfer
  evidence.

Evidence lane and capacity are orthogonal. The same protocol geometry produces the
same numerical design-capacity result in every lane. A lane can authorize a stronger
claim, but it cannot add or subtract benchmark points.

## Typed states

`exact`, `interval`, `conditional`, `unknown`, `absent`, and `not_scoreable` remain
distinct. Numeric zero is reserved for measured or logically structural zero.
Unknown is never replaced by zero, a midpoint, or a synthetic matrix.

## Source binding

Every displayed field requires an exact source object, content hash, locator,
projection pointer, and value digest. A field may remain known only when every
declared binding has a receipted executable operator that reproduces it from the
frozen source node. Interpretive, manual, or unresolved extraction is downgraded to
typed unknown while preserving source identifiers, locators, and the exact reason;
source-byte hash replay does not validate or promote the curator's interpretation.
A citation proves only the cited field. It does not authorize assay operators,
covariance, participant-event lineage, assignment counts, linked outcomes,
moderators, transport coordinates, accessibility, or demonstrated model yield unless
those fields are independently bound.

## Hard boundaries

- Planned events and realized events are separate objects.
- Enrollment is not an assayed or retained denominator.
- A modality name is not observation resolution.
- Different people or dates are not a joint multimodal event.
- A portal is not successful reanalysis.
- Retrospective subgroups are not prospective personalization.
- Fixed-probability moderator analysis is heterogeneity, not a state-dependent policy.
- Natural perturbations are not randomized operators.
- Raw bytes, feature counts, and duplicated rows do not create information.

## Promotion

Implemented, tested, packaged, remotely readable, biologically calibrated,
independently reviewed, and public-rank eligible are separate gates. Promotions bind
the exact benchmark version, authority hashes, protocol facts, compiler, corpus,
evidence lane, chart bytes, and review receipt. Corrections create a superseding
record rather than rewriting history.

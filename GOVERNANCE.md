# Governance

## Roles

- Maintainers own code health and release engineering.
- The Methods Council owns constructs, family formulas, Level-1 authority
  targets, task suites, and major versions.
- Evidence curators extract source spans and evidence cells.
- Independent raters review cells without seeing the other rating where feasible.
- Adjudicators resolve disagreements and cannot adjudicate their own study.
- A release manager records gates and the immutable release decision.
- Community challengers can submit exact source-located corrections.

## ANI-affiliated studies

ANI studies are included, never privileged. Their labels and affiliations are masked during formula calibration and initial rating where possible. ANI-affiliated contributors abstain from adjudicating their own evidence. ANI studies are excluded from fitting weights, platform anchors, or the ideal reference. A methods change that moves an ANI study materially requires a public impact report and independent vote.

## Change control

- Patch: source correction without formula/schema semantics.
- Minor: compatible study, platform, task, or chart addition.
- Major: construct, evidence state, family, formula, Level-1 authority, or
  capability-level change.

Released assessment packets are immutable. Corrections create a new release
with machine-readable supersession links. A scalar leaderboard is not a current
governance object; family-specific or Pareto comparisons require their own
source-completeness and release decision.

## Challenges

A challenge names the study, evidence cell or formula symbol, current value, proposed value, exact source span, evidence state, and expected impact. Status is visible as received, validated, second rating, adjudicated, accepted, rejected, or superseded.
